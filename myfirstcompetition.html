Your First BJJ Competition: A Guide for Restigouche Dojo Athletes

Welcome, Restigouche Dojo Warriors! Congratulations on taking the step to compete in your first Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) tournament. Competing is a fantastic way to test your skills, gain experience, and grow as a martial artist. This guide will help you prepare both physically and mentally for the challenge ahead.

Guide to Preparing for Your First BJJ Competition

1. Set Clear Goals

Understand why you’re competing. Is it to test your skills, gain experience, or simply push your boundaries? Having a clear purpose will help you stay focused and motivated.

2. Develop a Game Plan

A game plan is essential for success. This isn’t just a vague idea but a detailed strategy for every possible scenario.

  • Starting Position: Decide whether you’ll aim for a takedown or pull guard.
  • Top Positions: Know your go-to moves for passing guard and maintaining control.
  • Bottom Positions: Have specific sweeps and escapes drilled into muscle memory.
  • Submissions: Focus on a few high-percentage submissions that you can execute well.
  • Defense: Be prepared to defend against common attacks and recover from bad positions.

3. Train Smart

Consistency is key. Regular training sessions will improve your techniques, stamina, and overall fitness.

  • Spar with Different Partners: This will expose you to various styles and body types.
  • Drill, Drill, Drill: Repetition builds proficiency. Focus on the techniques in your game plan.
  • Conditioning: Incorporate cardio, strength training, and flexibility exercises. Jump rope, running, and bodyweight exercises are excellent choices.

4. Mental Preparation

Your mindset is just as important as your physical preparation.

  • Relax and Breathe: Learn to control your breathing. Practice deep diaphragm breathing: inhale through your nose for four seconds, fill your lungs, and exhale through your mouth for eight seconds.
  • Visualization: Picture your matches in your mind. See yourself executing your game plan, staying calm, and handling any challenges. Always visualize positive outcomes.
  • Stay Positive: Replace negative thoughts with positive affirmations. Believe in your training and your ability to perform well.

5. Understand the Rules

Familiarize yourself with the tournament’s rules and point system. Know what submissions are allowed at your belt level and how points are awarded:

  • Takedown or Throw: 2 points
  • Sweep: 2 points
  • Knee on Belly: 2 points
  • Passing the Guard: 3 points
  • Mount Position: 4 points

6. Gear Up

Ensure you have the correct gear:

  • Gi: Make sure your gi is IBJJF-legal or meets the specific tournament’s requirements. It should be clean and in good condition.
  • Belt: Wear a belt that corresponds to your rank.
  • Mouthguard: Protect your teeth.
  • Extras: Bring a spare gi, water, snacks, and any other personal items you may need.

7. Warm-Up Properly

A good warm-up helps calm your mind and prepare your body.

  • Routine: Perform a familiar warm-up routine that you do before training sessions.
  • Focus: Concentrate on your movements and breathing, not the upcoming matches.

8. Nutrition and Hydration

Your performance depends on how well you fuel your body.

  • Hydrate: Drink plenty of water leading up to the competition.
  • Eat Smart: Consume a balanced diet rich in proteins, carbohydrates, and healthy fats. On the day of the competition, eat light, easily digestible foods.

9. On Competition Day

Arrive early, stay calm, and trust your training. Use your breathing techniques and visualization to stay relaxed. Believe in your preparation and follow your game plan.

10. Post-Competition Reflection

No matter the outcome, every competition is a learning experience. Seek feedback from your coach and teammates, reflect on what went well and what didn’t, and use this to guide your future training.
